# Privacy and Legal Requirements

## Privacy Requirements

| ID | Requirement | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| PR-01 | The system shall collect only the personal data required for academy operations. | Data minimization. |
| PR-02 | The system shall protect child-related data with stricter access control than standard user data. | Safeguard minors. |
| PR-03 | The system shall restrict medical and emergency-contact information to authorized roles. | Protect sensitive records. |
| PR-04 | The system shall support consent-aware handling of personal and sensitive information where required. | Respect lawful processing. |
| PR-05 | The system shall support retention rules for records and audit history. | Limit unnecessary storage. |
| PR-06 | The system shall support controlled deletion or anonymization where legally and operationally appropriate. | Reduce privacy risk. |
| PR-07 | The system shall allow users to review only the personal data they are permitted to access. | Prevent privacy leakage. |
| PR-08 | The system shall keep a clear record of privacy-sensitive changes. | Support accountability. |

## Legal Requirements

| ID | Requirement | Relevance |
| --- | --- | --- |
| LR-01 | The system shall support GDPR-aligned principles for data minimization, integrity, confidentiality, transparency, and accountability. | Personal data governance. |
| LR-02 | The system shall support Singapore PDPA-aligned handling of consent, purpose limitation, and protection obligations. | Regional compliance. |
| LR-03 | The system shall respect copyright and intellectual property obligations for text, media, and documents. | Content handling. |
| LR-04 | The system shall support record retention and lawful deletion rules. | Data lifecycle management. |
| LR-05 | The system shall provide privacy notices and policy references suitable for informed use. | Transparency. |
| LR-06 | The system shall preserve audit history even when user accounts are removed. | Accountability and evidentiary records. |

## Privacy and Legal Notes

- Privacy obligations are highest for children, medical records, and emergency contacts.
- Legal obligations inform design, but implementation detail is deferred to later tasks.
