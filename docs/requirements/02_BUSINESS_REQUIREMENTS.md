# Business Requirements

| ID | Business Requirement | Business Value | Priority |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| BR-01 | The system shall centralize management of people, roles, and relationships for the academy. | Removes spreadsheet drift and duplicated recordkeeping. | Must |
| BR-02 | The system shall protect personal, medical, attendance, and performance information. | Reduces safeguarding and privacy risk. | Must |
| BR-03 | The system shall support role-specific workflows for administrators, coaches, parents, and artists. | Improves usability and reduces user error. | Must |
| BR-04 | The system shall capture attendance, performance, injury, and medical information in a consistent way. | Enables reliable operational records. | Must |
| BR-05 | The system shall maintain immutable audit history for important actions. | Supports accountability and investigations. | Must |
| BR-06 | The system shall provide search, filtering, and export for operational records. | Speeds up daily management and reporting. | Should |
| BR-07 | The system shall provide dashboards and reports that summarize academy activity. | Helps users monitor performance and wellbeing. | Must |
| BR-08 | The system shall support secure profile management and record updates. | Reduces support overhead and keeps data current. | Must |
| BR-09 | The system shall support data retention, controlled deletion, and privacy obligations. | Reduces legal and compliance risk. | Must |
| BR-10 | The system shall remain extensible for future expansion without redesign. | Protects long-term value and maintainability. | Should |
| BR-11 | The system shall control account onboarding rather than allow open public self-registration. | Reduces unauthorized access risk. | Must |

## Business Requirement Notes

- Business requirements describe value and outcomes, not implementation steps.
- Any requirement involving children, medical data, or administrative powers is treated
  as high sensitivity.
